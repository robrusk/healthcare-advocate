### 4. Legal Basis for Appeal
Under ERISA § 503 and 29 C.F.R. § 2560.503-1, plan participants are entitled to a full and fair review of any adverse benefit determination. The denial notice must specify the exact plan provision relied upon and the clinical criteria applied. We hereby request that this appeal be reviewed by a qualified medical professional with expertise in the relevant specialty — not merely an administrative reviewer.

This plan is subject to ERISA and must comply with its claims procedure requirements, including the right to review and copy all documents relevant to the claim at no cost.

### 5. Clinical Argument
The denied service — {{service}} — is medically necessary for the treatment of {{diagnosis}}. Medical necessity is established by:

- The treating physician's clinical judgment, as documented in the medical records
- Published clinical guidelines supporting this treatment for this diagnosis
- The patient's individual clinical circumstances, which are not adequately captured by algorithmic review

The insurer's reviewing physician (if any) has not examined the patient and lacks the clinical context available to the treating team. A determination made without individualized review of the patient's clinical history does not meet the standard for a "full and fair review" required under ERISA.
