Your Privacy

We built this tool because families fighting the healthcare system shouldn't have to give up their privacy to get help.

Last updated: May 2026 — If we ever change how data flows through this tool, this page gets updated first.

The short version

If you close this browser tab, there is no record anywhere that you ever used this site. Not on our servers. Not in a database. Not in an analytics dashboard. Gone.

No Accounts. No Tracking. No Database.

We don't ask you to sign up. We don't know who you are. We don't store anything on our servers between sessions. We have no database of users, documents, or activity.

Where Your Document Goes

When you upload a denial letter or medical bill, here is the complete path your document travels:

📱 Your browser — reads the file and converts it to a secure format in memory
↓ sent over HTTPS (encrypted)
⚡ Our Cloudflare Worker — a stateless passthrough proxy
Receives the request and forwards it. No logging. No storage. Forgets it immediately.
↓ forwarded over HTTPS
🤖 Anthropic's Claude AI — analyzes your document
↓ result returned
📱 Your browser — displays the analysis

That is the entire path. Nothing is saved along the way. No files are uploaded to a storage bucket. No intermediate copies are created. The document lives in your browser's memory for the duration of your session and nowhere else.

Anthropic Does Not Train AI Models on Your Data

Your document is processed by Anthropic's Claude AI. Per Anthropic's API terms:

  • ✅  Your data is not used to train any AI model
  • ✅  Your data is not retained for product improvement
  • ⚠️  Inputs may be held for up to 30 days for automated abuse monitoring — no human reviews your document unless it is specifically flagged by that automated system

We are pursuing Anthropic's Zero Data Retention agreement as a nonprofit, which would eliminate even this window. We'll update this page when that's in place.

Standard Hosting Metadata

Like any website on the internet, Cloudflare (our hosting and CDN provider) records standard request metadata when you visit:

  • 📋  Your IP address
  • 🕐  The time of your visit
  • 🔗  The URL you visited
  • ✅  Never the contents of your document

This is standard CDN logging — the same metadata collected by every website you visit. Cloudflare uses it for infrastructure purposes (abuse prevention, traffic routing). We do not use it to identify users or analyze behavior.

No Analytics. No Ad Networks. No Cookies From Us.

We do not use Google Analytics, Facebook Pixel, or any other third-party tracking script. We don't have ads. We don't sell anything. There is nothing in the app that sends your browsing behavior anywhere.

Cloudflare may set infrastructure cookies (for bot detection and load balancing). These are set by Cloudflare's infrastructure — not by our application — and contain no personal information about you or your documents.

Nothing Saved in Your Browser Either

Your uploaded document and the AI analysis live only in your browser's temporary session memory (React state). We do not save anything to localStorage, IndexedDB, or browser cookies. When you close the tab or navigate away, everything is cleared.

Your browser may cache the app itself (HTML, JavaScript, CSS) so it loads faster next time — but it never caches your documents or analysis results.

One Exception: Email

Important

If you email us at office@healthcareadvocate.org, that email is stored in our email system and read by a real person. Don't email us your denial letter or medical bill unless you want it kept on file. The app itself does not require you to email us anything — it works entirely without contact.

Are We HIPAA Compliant?

HIPAA applies to health plans, healthcare providers, and their vendors — not to tools that patients use themselves. You can share your own health information with anyone you choose; HIPAA doesn't restrict that. We are a patient advocacy tool — you're using us to handle your own records, not giving a healthcare vendor access to them.

The honest answer — HIPAA doesn't apply to us because we're patient-side, not provider-side — is more accurate than a "HIPAA Compliant" badge. Most such badges in the AI healthcare space are marketing language, not legal conclusions.

That said, we designed this app to meet the same standards HIPAA would require: minimum necessary data, no retention, no secondary use. The architecture enforces it technically, not just by policy.

Our Commitment

This is a volunteer project. We have no investors, no advertisers, and no revenue. There is no business model that benefits from your data. We are exploring nonprofit incorporation — the core commitment is already in place regardless: patients first, always. Free forever for the family. No advertising, no data selling, no conflicts of interest.

We will never sell your data. We will never use it to train AI. We will never monetize it in any form. If any of that ever changes — which would require amending our nonprofit charter — this page gets updated before the change takes effect.
